Tag Plugins in Hexo

Tag plugins are different from post tags. They are ported from Octopress and provide a useful way for you to quickly add specific content to your posts.

Although you can write your posts in any formats, but the tag plugins will always be available and syntax remains the same.

Tag plugins should not be wrapped inside Markdown syntax, e.g. []({% post_path lorem-ipsum %}) is not supported.

Block Quote

Perfect for adding quotes to your post, with optional author, source and title information.

Examples

No arguments. Plain blockquote.

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. Pellentesque hendrerit lacus ut purus iaculis feugiat. Sed nec tempor elit, quis aliquam neque. Curabitur sed diam eget dolor fermentum semper at eu lorem.

When you editing in a markdown editor such as Typora, will see that the example paragraph above is not properly rendered. So remember, Tag Plugins are formatting tools coming along with Hexo. In my own production use case of Hexo + Butterfly + Typora , I’d rather use markdown grammar, > + Space + quoted text, which would provide with you the the immediate preview of your post in editing.

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. Pellentesque hendrerit lacus ut purus iaculis feugiat. Sed nec tempor elit, quis aliquam neque. Curabitur sed diam eget dolor fermentum semper at eu lorem.
